Our Position

One platform. Done properly.

The big management companies list on five platforms simultaneously. That's their hedge. This is the deliberate opposite.

Every booking platform has its own search algorithm. Those algorithms reward the same signals: review volume, booking frequency, response rate, conversion rate. When you split bookings across Airbnb, Booking.com, and VRBO, you're not amplifying those signals — you're diluting them.

You end up looking like a new listing on three platforms simultaneously, rather than an established, trusted host on one.

The compounding effect runs in reverse too. Each review on a single platform makes the next booking more likely. Each booking improves your conversion rate. A better conversion rate tells the algorithm to show your listing to more people. This loop only works when your signal is concentrated, not scattered.

Argument supported by data from Jason Baxter, short-term rental consultant. Read on LinkedIn →

1

Reviews compound on a single platform

Split across four channels, you're always a new listing. Concentrated on one, you build ranking that gets harder for competitors to displace.

2

Superhost status is Airbnb-native

4.8+ rating, 90%+ response rate, under 1% cancellations — all measured on Airbnb alone. It is the single biggest organic ranking driver on the platform.

3

Algorithm depth beats breadth

Airbnb's search algorithm rewards engagement signals that don't transfer between platforms. A host optimised deeply for Airbnb will consistently outrank one spread thinly across three OTAs.

Summer coast traffic

Hunstanton, Heacham, and the coast drive strong summer demand. Minimum stays and seasonal pricing have to be calibrated for this — not applied generically.

Sandringham proximity

The Sandringham estate creates a specific pull for certain guests — and certain expectations around property standard that differ from the coastal market.

Burnham Market premium

Properties here command a meaningful premium that has to be reflected in pricing and listing presentation — it doesn't happen automatically.

ac_unit

Quiet winter months

PE31 winters are quiet. Gap-fill strategy, long-stay pricing, and remote-worker demand become relevant from October. Property owners need to plan for this.

Second-home council tax

A properly run Airbnb listing meeting the 140-day threshold can qualify a property for business rates relief instead of double council tax — a significant financial difference.
